Concession at Full Strength

The consortium graveyard.

Blockchain trade finance backed by the world's largest banks failed three times in two years. The bear case has tombstones.

Conditional analysis

This series argues from stated assumptions. The purpose is not to promise outcomes, but to show what the math and structure would require if adoption conditions are met — and to price the probability that they are met at all.

This part has not yet been published. The series contents are visible from day one by design; each part is written in dedicated sessions and published here when it meets the framework's sourcing standard.

What this part will cover

  • we.trade (dissolved 2022), Marco Polo Network (insolvent 2023), Contour (wound down 2023) — an autopsy of each.
  • The failure causes: governance, incentive misalignment, permissioned-network liquidity islands, and — critically — the absence of legal recognition for electronic transferable records during their lifetimes.
  • The differentiation argument, only as far as it honestly goes: MLETR and the ETDA change the legal substrate; public-chain neutrality changes the incentive structure; TFDi participation changes distribution.
  • A plain statement of which failure causes are cured by these changes — and which are not.
